## Why callers don’t call back (especially for barbershops and salons)

When a business doesn’t answer, callers don’t all wait around.

According to CallRail, when businesses don’t answer:

* 21% of consumers immediately call another business
* 24% switch to online chat
* 42% leave a voicemail
  Source: [CallRail survey summary](https://www.callrail.com/blog/missed-calls-cost-businesses-more-than-ever)

That “call another business” behavior is exactly why barbershops and salons get hit so hard. The customer is ready now, and switching is easy.

## Voicemail isn’t a safety net

A lot of owners assume: “If it’s important, they’ll leave a voicemail.”

But voicemail behavior has been declining. CBS News reported (citing Vonage data) that about 80% of callers don’t bother leaving voicemails.
Source: [CBS News on voicemail decline](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/is-it-time-to-hang-up-on-voicemail/)

Even if your business gets some voicemails, the bigger problem remains: many callers won’t wait for callbacks. They’ll just book elsewhere.

## The ROI formula is simple

Here’s the easiest way to estimate missed-call revenue loss:

Lost revenue = missed calls × booking rate × average service value

Where:

* missed calls = inbound calls × missed call rate
* booking rate = how many callers would have booked if you answered
* average service value = your average ticket

CallRail reports that, on average, 28% of calls to businesses go unanswered.
Source: [CallRail missed call rate](https://www.callrail.com/blog/missed-calls-costing-your-business)

## Example math for a barbershop vs a salon (CZK)

Let’s use real public prices so this stays grounded:

* Barbershop example: “Classic haircut” listed at 699 CZK.
  Source: [Barbershop Urban pricelist](https://barbershopurban.cz/en/pricelist/)
* Salon example: women’s cut + wash + blow-dry listed from 1,400 CZK.
  Source: [Trichomania pricelist](https://www.trichomania.cz/prices.html)

Now assume a shop gets 20 inbound calls/day.

1. Missed calls/day
   20 × 0.28 = 5.6 missed calls/day
   Source for 0.28: [CallRail missed call rate](https://www.callrail.com/blog/missed-calls-costing-your-business)

2. Booking rate
   For a conservative benchmark, Nextiva cites that 37% of digital marketing leads convert on their call.
   Source: [Nextiva on missed calls](https://www.nextiva.com/blog/whats-the-cost-of-missed-calls.html)

(For barbershops and salons, the real booking rate from calls can be higher because callers are often ready to book — but we’ll keep it conservative here.)

3. Lost bookings/day
   5.6 × 0.37 = 2.072 bookings/day

4. Lost revenue/day

* Barbershop: 2.072 × 699 = 1,448.328 CZK/day
* Salon: 2.072 × 1,400 = 2,900.8 CZK/day

If you want a simple “per month” estimate (26 working days):

* Barbershop: 1,448.328 × 26 = 37,656.528 CZK/month
* Salon: 2,900.8 × 26 = 75,420.8 CZK/month

Again, the exact numbers will vary, but the model is the point: in high-intent industries, missed calls leak revenue every single day.

## The hidden cost nobody counts: missed calls steal time from paying customers

This is what makes the problem so frustrating in service businesses:

You’re with a client (who is literally paying for your time), and the phone rings.

* If you pick up: your current client is paying for that interruption with their time and experience.
* If you don’t pick up: the caller often books somewhere else.

I lived the same tradeoff when I taught golf lessons. Mid-lesson, someone calls to book. If I answer, I’m stealing time from the person in front of me. If I don’t answer, I might lose the next client entirely.

Barbershops and salons have this problem all day.

## What you can do today (without hiring anyone)

### 1) Measure your missed calls for 7 days

Count:

* inbound calls/day
* missed calls/day
* when they happen (peak hours, evenings, weekends)

Once you see the number, the ROI becomes obvious.

### 2) List the top 10 caller questions

Most calls cluster around:

* “Do you have availability today?”
* “How much is X?”
* “How long does it take?”
* “Where are you / where do I park?”
* “Can I reschedule?”

The faster these are answered, the fewer interruptions you have while working.

### 3) Stop relying on voicemail as plan A

Voicemail can exist, but it shouldn’t be your strategy.
